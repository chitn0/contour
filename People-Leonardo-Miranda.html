The History Lesson

These pills are amphetamines which were created in the year 1933 by Lazar Edeleanu and were sold under the name ‘Benzedrine’ or Benniesfor short.

After in 1943 the US army was issuing ‘pep pills’ for soldiers to boost morale and fight battle fatigue. These were pure amphetamines and by the end of the World War 2 they had shipped over 150 million. The Japanese and Germans at the time were also using these pep pills and even the Fuhrer himself would get daily injectionsof amphetamines.

The science behind the pills is that people with ADHD tend to have lower levels of dopamine which is a key chemical in our brains ‘reward centre’. The constant lack of dopamine leads suffers in a struggle to find constant stimulation thus making it incredibly difficult to concentrate on the task at hand. An example is having to do an assignment but constantly pushing it back to attend to every little notification from Facebook or text or even searching up in google what the net worth of Simon Cowell is. It’s not so much procrastination but rather an extremely short attention span. What amphetamines does is increase the amount of dopamine that is released in the brain and push them to brain reactors in order to stimulate the cells so that it doesn’t let minor distractions cause you to lose focus.Amphetamine’s at a molecular scope looks like this; it’s a group of carbon and 3 hydrogen atoms called a ‘methyl group’ If you add just 1 more ‘methyl group’ onto the end of amphetamine molecule it turns in to the dangerous substance methamphetamine, making it a fine line between a studious citizens to a total tweaker.

Sounding too good to be true? Well like everything there are side effects such as dizziness, headaches, anxiety, nausea and nervousness. In most extreme cases it can cause motor tics, uncontrolled movements of the face, mouth, or tongue, skin rashes and heart palpitations.

The difference in Modafinil, Ritalin and Adderall is down to its concentrated dosage percent. Modafinil being the lowest dosage has no addiction potential. However Ritalin being more potent and Adderall even higher in dosage, it can lead to the user becoming dependant on the drug. Many believe it being a gateway drug as they grow accustom to its effect and no longer stimulated, there’s the likelihood of abuse of more potent amphetamines or drugs.
